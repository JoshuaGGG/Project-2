Common Cybersecurity Attacks

Various devices connected to the internet, such as computers, phones, gaming systems, and Bluetooth devices, can be exploited in cyber attacks.

Malware

Malware is the most common type of cyberattacks and is often displayed like most software viruses that will corrupt or take over a system.

Phishing

Phishing is an attack where a person impersonates another person’s identity. The social engineering act will be committed by the intruder sending what the trusted reader believes to think is an email. In reality this is a fake email clicking the direct attachment will lead to clicking a malicious link which therefore will provide access to the invader.

Spear Phishing

This attack is very similar to phishing but instead the hacker gains insight on its prey ready to strike through by also impersonating something that isn’t true.

Man In the Middle (MITM)

Man in the middle attack is being an eavesdropper in a conversation of electronic texts and intercepting to confuse them.

Trojans

Trojans is a type of malware that when entered into a system perceives to look like something safe but at the end releases all of the malicious software.

More Cyber Threats

Denial Of Service (DDos)

Denial of service attack includes the overwhelming factor of the systems being overrun by many incoming requests to the bandwidth and resources a system has. This will cause the software to shut down on demand and will leave the hacker with an easy to open to information.

Ransomware

Ransomware is the encryption of data on the victims data which in exchange asks for an ambitious return of a ransom request for the user to gain access back.

SQL Injection

Structured Query Language or SQL is the act of manipulating a query with malicious code in the search box revealing immaculate details on the victim.

Watering Hole attack

A watering hole attack is done by targeting websites that are frequently used by the victim. Then those websites are filled to the brim with spiteful malware conquering personal information.

Password Attack

This type of attack includes a hacker unraveling a password through a series of tools used. The type of password attacks can be brute force attacks, dictionary attacks, and keylogger attacks.
